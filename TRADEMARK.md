# Trademark Policy

*Warp* and *Webspinner* are trademarks of The Webspinner Foundation. The Apache License 2.0 governs the code in this repository. The Apache License does not grant rights to the Foundation's trademarks; §6 of the license says so explicitly. This document specifies what use of the names is permitted.

## Why the trademark is held separately

Stewardship of the architecture lives in the trademark. Sovereignty of the architecture lives in the open license. Apache 2.0 makes the code freely forkable, modifiable, and redistributable — that is the point. The trademark is what ensures forks that drift from the Foundation Pledge cannot trade on the Foundation's name. See `WARP-CANON.md` §11 (the Pledge) for the alignment standard.

## Permitted use

You may, without separate permission:

- **Refer to Warp the architecture** in writing, presentations, technical documentation, journalism, academic work, and the like. *"This system implements the Warp Capability Bus protocol."* *"We surveyed Warp deployments across small businesses."* *"Per the Warp specification..."*
- **State factual compatibility.** *"Compatible with Warp protocol version 1.0."* *"Implements the WRAG retrieval pattern."*
- **Cite the Webspinner Foundation** as the steward of the architecture in attribution, references, and bibliographies.
- **Use the names in URLs, package names, and identifiers** when the artifact itself is a faithful reference implementation that meets the Foundation Pledge — and only then.
- **Reproduce the names** in unmodified copies of this repository's documentation, manuscript excerpts, and Foundation-published material.

## Restricted use

You may not, without prior written permission of the Foundation:

- **Name a fork *Warp* or use *Warp* as the primary name of a derivative product or service**, especially when the derivative diverges from the Foundation Pledge in ch. 26 of the manuscript.
- **Claim Foundation endorsement, alignment, or certification** for software, services, or organizations that have not been recognized as such by the Foundation.
- **Use the name *Webspinner* in a company, product, or service name** that suggests affiliation with the Foundation.
- **Use the trademarks in advertising, marketing, or promotional material** in ways that imply Foundation sponsorship or partnership.
- **Register domains, social-media handles, or trademarks** that incorporate *Warp* or *Webspinner* in ways that confuse or mislead about origin.
- **Use the trademarks in connection with applications the Foundation Pledge refuses** — population-scale behavioral targeting, mass surveillance, autonomous-weapons targeting, or any use the Pledge specifically forbids.

## Forks and derivatives

Forking the code is welcome and expected — it is an Apache-licensed reference implementation. A fork should:

- Choose a name distinct from *Warp* and *Webspinner*.
- State, in its README and NOTICE, that it is a derivative of Warp and that *Warp* is a trademark of the Webspinner Foundation.
- Not represent itself as the Foundation's reference implementation unless it is.
- Honor the underlying Apache 2.0 obligations (NOTICE preservation, change attribution).

A fork that meets the Foundation Pledge and follows the Foundation's specifications faithfully may apply for recognition as an aligned implementation. The recognition process will be published with the next architecture release.

## Removal

If the Foundation determines that a use of *Warp* or *Webspinner* violates this policy, the Foundation will request removal. The architecture's openness depends on the Foundation's ability to defend the marks against misuse; the Foundation will defend them.

## Contact

Trademark questions, recognition applications, and removal requests: when the Foundation publishes its contact channels with the architecture release, they will be listed here. Until then, address inquiries through the Foundation's public-facing page (link to come) or via issues on this repository.
